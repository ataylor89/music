In the table below, I'll define some important terms from music theory

## Music vocabulary

| Word | Definition |
| ---- | ---------- |
| Counterpoint | (1) A musical technique in which two or more melodies are played simultaneously (2) Interwoven melodies in a musical composition (source: Scripps National Spelling Bee) |
| Fugue | A fugue is a musical form that is based on (or built around) a subject. The subject is the primary melodic theme. It is often introduced at the very beginning. The piece then explores the subject, develops it, repeats it, and it often introduces one or more melodies that complement the subject. Some examples of fugues are Art of Fugue by J.S. Bach, the 48 different fugues in Book 1 and Book 2 of Well Tempered Clavier by J.S. Bach, Passacaglia and Fugue in C minor by J.S. Bach, Toccata and Fugue in D minor by J.S. Bach, Fugue in G Minor by J.S. Bach, the second movement of Beethoven's seventh symphony, and the fourth movement of Mozart's fourty first ("Jupiter") symphony. |
| Harmony | A musical technique in which two or more notes are played simultaneously |
| Melody | A sequence of notes; a sequence of notes that creates a musical tune |
| Motif | A motif is just a theme; in my opinion, the two words are synonymous and interchangeable, although they might have slightly different connotations |
| Theme | A musical idea that develops or repeats throughout a piece |
